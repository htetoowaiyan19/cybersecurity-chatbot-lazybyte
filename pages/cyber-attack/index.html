What is a Cyber Attack?

A cyber attack is an intentional exploitation of systems, networks, or technologies to steal, disrupt, or destroy information. Here's a breakdown of common types.

Phishing

Fake emails or links used to trick users into giving away sensitive data.

Malware

Malicious software like viruses, worms, or trojans that harm systems or steal data.

Ransomware

Encrypts your files and demands money to unlock them.

DDoS Attack

Overwhelms a system with traffic, causing downtime and disruption.

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM)

Hackers secretly intercept communication between two people to steal data like passwords or credit card info.

SQL-Injection

Attackers insert malicious SQL commands into a website’s input fields to gain unauthorized access to databases — allowing them to view, edit, or delete information.

Brute Force Attack

Attackers try many combinations of passwords very quickly to break into user accounts — especially weak or reused passwords.

Social Engineering

If your password is leaked in a data breach, attackers try that same password on other sites — assuming you reused it — to hijack your accounts.

DNS Spoofing

Hackers manipulate the Domain Name System (DNS) to redirect users to fake websites — even if they typed the correct address.
